George Brown College
George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology is a publicly funded college located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Established in 1967, it has grown to become one of Canada's leading institutions for practical, hands-on education, offering full-time, part-time, and continuing education programs across various disciplines.

The college operates three campuses in downtown Toronto:

St. James Campus – Home to business, creative arts, and technology programs, featuring industry-standard facilities and innovative learning spaces.
Casa Loma Campus – The hub for construction, engineering, and technology programs, providing students with hands-on training using cutting-edge technology.
Waterfront Campus – Focused on health sciences programs, this campus includes modern simulation labs for nursing, dental, and health-related studies.
George Brown College is known for its strong industry connections, ensuring students gain real-world experience through co-op placements, internships, and applied research projects. The college offers more than 160 programs across various fields, including:

Business & Finance
Information Technology & Computer Science
Engineering & Construction
Health Sciences & Nursing
Hospitality & Culinary Arts
Creative Arts & Design
With a diverse student population of over 30,000 full-time students and an additional 60,000 continuing education students, George Brown College embraces inclusivity and innovation. It consistently ranks among the top research colleges in Canada, focusing on projects that drive advancements in technology, healthcare, and sustainability.

The institution partners with global businesses, startups, and government organizations, ensuring that graduates are well-prepared for the job market. Over 90% of George Brown graduates find employment within six months of completing their programs.
